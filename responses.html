4. What are your greatest strengths and weaknesses?

    My greatest strength is my attention to detail—especially when working with data.
    I consistently double-check my queries and reports to ensure accuracy, and I always document my steps. I’m also strong at communicating complex findings clearly, even to non-technical stakeholders.

    As for a weakness, I used to spend too much time trying to perfect every analysis, which affected my efficiency.
    I've learned to set clear priorities and time-box tasks, which helps me balance precision with productivity.

5. Describe a challenging project you've worked on and how you handled it.

    During my internship, I was tasked with analyzing sales performance across multiple regions using inconsistent Excel files.
    The data had missing fields, different formats, and some duplicates.
    It was overwhelming at first, but I developed a step-by-step plan: I standardized the format using Excel functions, cleaned the data with Power Query, and then imported it into Tableau for visualization.
    After a week of careful work, I presented the findings to the team, helping them identify two underperforming regions and leading to a new sales strategy.
    The experience taught me a lot about data hygiene and resilience under pressure.
